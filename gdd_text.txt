Executive SummaryGround &amp; Pound: Chronicles of the Cage is a browser-based, text-driven MMA career RPG. You start as an unknown amateur with nothing but raw potential and a gym membership, and you fight your way through five fictional promotion tiers to the apex: the Global Combat Series (GCS) world championship.The game is built around one compelling fantasy — building a fighter from zero, making every training decision, picking your fights carefully, managing your body, and eventually standing in the biggest cage in the world with a belt. Every mechanic serves that fantasy.The MVP is single-player only. Multiplayer is scoped for v2.0. The energy system is the core pacing and monetisation mechanism — players always have something meaningful to spend Energy on, and purchasing Energy Packs is never blocked by timers.Game TitleGround &amp; Pound: Chronicles of the CageGenreText-Based MMA Career RPGPlatformWeb Browser (Desktop &amp; Mobile responsive)Target Audience18–40; MMA fans; sports management fans; idle/incremental RPG playersMVP ScopeSingle-player career mode; 5 promotion tiers; full fight and training systems; no PvPMonetisationFree-to-play; Energy Packs; Premium subscription ($9.99/month)MultiplayerScoped for v2.0 — gym rivalries, online rankings, live matchmakingNo LevelsProgression is driven entirely by Overall Rating — no separate XP/level barNo CooldownsNo time gates on fights; limits are daily fight caps and stat economics2. Core Design Philosophy2.1 The Three RulesEvery design decision in this document was made against three rules. When mechanics conflict, these rules resolve the conflict in order.Rule 1 — Energy Packs must always have somewhere to go. No timer, no cooldown, no gate may block a player who has Energy from spending it on something meaningful. The game&apos;s monetisation lives or dies on this rule.Rule 2 — Progress must be earned, not purchased. Buying Energy Packs lets you do more per day. It never lets you skip the opponent quality curve. A player who skips training and spams fights will get beaten up by players who trained. Money buys time, not power.Rule 3 — No separate level bar. The Overall Rating is the single progression number. It is derived from stats. It is visible, comparable, and meaningful. Adding a second XP bar creates confusion and dilutes the stat system.2.2 The Elegant GateMost games gate progression with locked doors: &apos;you need level 20 to enter this zone.&apos; Ground &amp; Pound uses a softer, more interesting gate: you can go wherever you want, but your opponents will reflect your readiness. Rush to Regional Pro at Overall 28 and you will lose consistently to fighters at Overall 45. The losses teach the lesson. The game never needs to say &apos;you cannot proceed&apos; — it just makes proceeding painful if you haven&apos;t prepared.The only hard gates are the signing fees (Iron cost to join a new promotion) and the Overall minimums for gym access. These are structural, not arbitrary — you can&apos;t train at an Elite gym if you&apos;re not an elite fighter yet. The gym quality gates the stat ceiling, which gates the Overall, which gates the gym. The loop is self-consistent.2.3 Design PillarsFighter Identity — A knockout artist&apos;s career plays nothing like a grappler&apos;s. Style choice shapes every outcome.Meaningful Decisions — Training allocation, opponent selection, strategy, and Odd Jobs all carry real consequences.Authentic Progression — The path mirrors real MMA. You earn nothing; you build everything.Session Richness — At every stage there are 4–6 different Energy-spending activities available. Sessions never feel like just clicking the same button.Respect for Spending — A paying player gets more sessions per day. They never get a stat advantage over a patient free player.3. As they age, training diminishing returns become slightly steeper and injury recovery lengthens. Peak years are 25–33. After 35 in-game, a Retirement Clock appears — the fighter can still compete but the narrative begins acknowledging the twilight.This creates urgency. Players feel pressure to achieve goals within a career window rather than grinding indefinitely. It also makes the Legacy Score (calculated at retirement) feel genuinely earned.12.4 Legacy Score &amp; New Game+On retirement, Legacy Score is calculated from championships won and defended, finish rate, opponent quality, Notoriety peak, career length, and activity. The score is permanent — it lives on a Hall of Fame wall. Starting a new fighter grants a small Legacy Bonus: a cosmetic title in the intro and a minor starting stat boost representing accumulated player knowledge.13.
